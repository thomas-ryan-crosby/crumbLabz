# Call Script: Solution Review Meeting
Version 1.0

## Purpose

This is the meeting where you present the Problem Definition Document and Solution One-Pager to the client. The goal is to confirm we understood their problem correctly, get buy-in on the proposed solution, and align on scope before development begins.

**Duration:** 30–45 minutes
**Platform:** Google Meet (Fireflies recording active)
**Attendees:** CrumbLabz lead + client stakeholder(s)

---

## Before the Call

- [ ] Review the AI-generated Problem Definition Document — edit for accuracy and tone
- [ ] Review the Solution One-Pager — ensure the proposed solution is realistic and scoped correctly
- [ ] Update document statuses to **Review** in the CRM
- [ ] Prepare to screenshare the documents during the call
- [ ] Check if the client added any new stakeholders to the meeting invite
- [ ] Name the calendar event: `[CompanyName] Solution Review`
- [ ] Have pricing notes ready (don't present yet unless they ask — focus on solution fit first)

## Call Structure

### 1. Opening (2 minutes)

**Re-establish rapport and set the agenda.**

> "Hey [First Name], good to see you again. Thanks for making the time. So today I want to walk you through two documents we put together based on our last conversation."

> "First, the Problem Definition — that's our structured understanding of your situation. I want to make sure we got it right. Then, the Solution One-Pager — that's our proposed approach to solving it."

> "The most important thing today is accuracy. If anything feels off or we missed something, I want to hear it. This is your chance to shape what we build before we start."

> "Fireflies is recording again — same as last time. Good with that?"

### 2. Problem Definition Walkthrough (8–10 minutes)

**Share your screen. Walk through each section.**

> "Let me pull up the Problem Definition Document. I'll walk you through it section by section."

**For each section, pause and check:**

- **Problem Statement:** "Does this capture the core issue? Anything you'd add or change?"
- **Current Workflow:** "Is this how it actually works? Any steps I'm missing?"
- **Pain Points:** "Did we get the big ones? Anything I missed that frustrates you?"
- **Impact Assessment:** "Do these numbers feel right? Too high? Too low?"
- **Success Criteria:** "Is this what 'solved' looks like to you?"

**Key phrases:**

- "Is that accurate?"
- "What would you change about how I described that?"
- "Am I missing anything important here?"
- "On a scale of 1–10, how well does this capture the problem?"

**Take notes on corrections. Don't defend — adjust.**

> "Great catch — I'll update that. Anything else on this section?"

### 3. Solution One-Pager Walkthrough (10–12 minutes)

**Transition from problem to solution.**

> "Okay, so now that we're aligned on the problem, let me show you what we're proposing to build."

**Walk through each section:**

- **Proposed Solution:** "Here's the high-level idea. Does this make sense as an approach?"
- **Key Features (MVP):** "These are the must-haves for version one. Do you agree with the priority? Anything missing that's critical? Anything here that's actually not important?"
- **Expected Benefits:** "These are the outcomes we're targeting. Do they match what matters to you?"
- **Technical Approach:** "I'll keep this simple — [describe in plain terms]. Any concerns about compatibility with your existing setup?"
- **Estimated Timeline:** "We're looking at roughly [X weeks] for the MVP. Does that timeline work for your needs?"

**Handle "can we add..." requests:**

> "That's a great idea. Let me note it down. For the MVP, I'd recommend we keep it focused on [core features] and add that in phase two. That way you get something working faster and we can iterate from there. Sound fair?"

### 4. Questions & Concerns (5 minutes)

**Open the floor.**

> "What questions do you have? Anything that concerns you or that you want me to clarify?"

**Common questions and how to handle them:**

**"How much will this cost?"**
> "Great question. For a project like this, we typically offer two options — a one-time build fee where you own the tool, or a monthly license where we host, maintain, and evolve it over time. Based on what we've discussed, I'd recommend [option] because [reason]. I'll send you a formal quote after we finalize scope today. Want me to walk through the rough numbers now, or would you prefer to see it in writing?"

**"Can we start sooner?"**
> "We can absolutely prioritize this. Let me check our current schedule and get back to you by [tomorrow/end of day] with a confirmed start date."

**"I need to run this by [someone else]."**
> "Totally understand. Want me to put together a brief summary you can share with them? Or would it be easier if we scheduled a quick call with them included?"

**"What if it doesn't work?"**
> "Fair question. That's exactly why we start with an MVP — a working version you can test before committing to the full build. You'll see it in action, give feedback, and we iterate from there. We don't ask for payment until you've seen the MVP and are happy with the direction."

### 5. Align on Next Steps (3–5 minutes)

**If they're ready to proceed:**

> "Great — so here's what happens next. I'll update the documents with the changes we discussed today and send you the final versions. Then we kick off development. You'll hear from me [every X days / weekly] with progress updates, and we'll schedule the MVP demo once it's ready."

> "I'll also send over the pricing details so you have everything in writing."

**If they need to think about it or consult others:**

> "No rush at all. I'll send you the updated documents so you can share them with [person]. Take the time you need. I'll follow up [next week / in a few days] to see where you're at. Sound good?"

**If scope changes are needed:**

> "I'll revise the Solution One-Pager based on what we discussed and send you an updated version by [date]. Once you approve that, we're ready to go."

## Key Principles

- **This meeting sells itself if the documents are good.** A sharp Problem Definition proves you listened. A clear Solution One-Pager proves you can solve it.
- **Don't oversell.** Confidence, not hype. "Here's what we'd build and why" beats "this is going to revolutionize your business."
- **Welcome pushback.** Every correction makes the final product better. Treat objections as collaboration, not resistance.
- **Scope discipline.** The MVP should be small and focused. "Yes, and we can add that in phase two" is your best friend.
- **Leave with a clear next step.** Never end with "let me know." End with "I'll do X by Y date, and we'll reconnect on Z."
