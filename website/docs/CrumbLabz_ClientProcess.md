# CrumbLabz Client Engagement Process
Version 1.0

## Overview

This document outlines the end-to-end process CrumbLabz follows from initial client contact through solution delivery and ongoing support. The process is designed to be lightweight, transparent, and fast — removing the friction that typically slows down custom software engagements.

---

## Phase 1: Client Outreach

### How Clients Find Us
- Inbound via website contact form (crumblabz.com)
- Referrals from existing clients
- Direct outreach (LinkedIn, email, networking)
- Content and social media presence

### Initial Response
- All inbound leads are captured in the CrumbLabz CRM and marked as "New"
- A team member responds within **24 hours** with a brief introduction and a link to schedule a discovery call
- The goal is to quickly qualify whether CrumbLabz is a good fit for the client's needs

### Qualification Criteria
- The client has an identifiable operational problem or inefficiency
- The problem can be addressed with a custom software tool
- The client has decision-making authority or direct access to someone who does
- The engagement aligns with CrumbLabz's capabilities and capacity

---

## Phase 2: Discovery Meeting

### Format
- **Platform:** Google Meet with AI meeting assistant (e.g., Fireflies.ai, Otter.ai, or similar)
- **Duration:** 30–60 minutes
- **Attendees:** CrumbLabz lead + client stakeholder(s)

### Agenda
1. **Introductions** — Brief background on CrumbLabz and how we work
2. **Problem Exploration** — The client walks through the pain point in their own words
   - What is the current process?
   - Where does it break down or create friction?
   - How much time/money does it cost?
   - Who is affected (team members, customers, etc.)?
3. **Context Gathering** — Understand the business environment
   - What tools or systems are currently in use?
   - Are there compliance, security, or integration requirements?
   - Has the client tried to solve this before? What happened?
4. **Desired Outcome** — What does success look like for the client?
5. **Next Steps** — Explain the problem definition and solution design process

### AI Recording & Transcript
- The meeting is recorded with the client's consent
- The AI assistant generates a full transcript and summary
- Key discussion points, pain points, and requirements are automatically extracted
- This recording serves as the primary input for the Problem Definition Document

---

## Phase 3: Problem Definition Document (Auto-Generated Draft)

### Generation Process
- The AI meeting transcript is processed to produce a structured Problem Definition Document
- A CrumbLabz team member reviews and refines the auto-generated draft for accuracy, tone, and completeness
- The document is finalized internally before being shared with the client

### Document Contents

1. **Client Overview** — Company name, industry, key contacts
2. **Problem Statement** — A clear, concise description of the operational problem
3. **Current Workflow** — Step-by-step breakdown of how the process works today, including tools used and manual steps involved
4. **Pain Points** — Specific inefficiencies, bottlenecks, error-prone steps, and time sinks identified during discovery
5. **Impact Assessment** — Estimated cost of the problem in terms of time, labor, errors, or missed opportunities
6. **Stakeholders** — Who is affected by the problem and who will use the solution
7. **Constraints & Requirements** — Technical, regulatory, or business constraints that the solution must respect
8. **Success Criteria** — Measurable outcomes that define a successful solution

### Delivery
- Delivered to the client as a PDF or shared document within **3–5 business days** of the discovery meeting
- The client is invited to review and flag any corrections before solution design begins

---

## Phase 4: Solution Design & Development Plan

Using the approved Problem Definition Document as the foundation, CrumbLabz produces two deliverables:

### A. Solution One-Pager

A concise, non-technical summary designed to be shared with decision-makers and stakeholders.

**Contents:**
- Problem summary (2–3 sentences)
- Proposed solution (plain-language description of what the tool does)
- Key benefits and expected improvements
- High-level technology approach (e.g., "web-based dashboard," "automated data pipeline")
- Estimated timeline to MVP
- Recommended commercial model (Solution Sale or Monthly License — see Business Model doc)

### B. Development Plan

A detailed technical plan for the CrumbLabz development team.

**Contents:**
1. **MVP Scope** — The minimum set of features required to solve the core problem
   - Feature list with priority ranking (Must Have / Nice to Have / Future)
   - User stories or workflow descriptions for each feature
2. **Technical Architecture** — High-level system design
   - Frontend, backend, database, and integration components
   - Hosting and infrastructure approach
   - Third-party services or APIs required
3. **Development Phases**
   - Phase 1: MVP (target: 1–2 weeks for most projects)
   - Phase 2: Refinements post-client feedback
   - Phase 3: Production hardening and handoff/deployment
4. **Assumptions & Dependencies** — What needs to be true for the plan to work (e.g., client provides API access, sample data, etc.)
5. **Risk Register** — Known risks and mitigation strategies

---

## Phase 5: Solution Review Meeting

### Format
- **Platform:** Google Meet with AI recording
- **Duration:** 30–45 minutes
- **Attendees:** CrumbLabz lead + client stakeholder(s)

### Agenda
1. **Walkthrough of the Solution One-Pager** — Ensure the client understands and agrees with the proposed approach
2. **MVP Scope Review** — Confirm the feature set for the initial build
3. **Timeline & Expectations** — Align on delivery dates and communication cadence
4. **Commercial Discussion** — Review pricing and recommended engagement model
5. **Client Feedback** — Capture any adjustments, concerns, or additional requirements
6. **Go/No-Go Decision** — Client confirms they want to proceed

### AI Recording
- Meeting is recorded and transcribed
- Action items and scope changes are extracted and used to update the core documents

---

## Phase 6: Document Refinement & Development

### Document Updates
- The Problem Definition, Solution One-Pager, and Development Plan are updated based on client feedback from the review meeting
- Final versions are shared with the client for written confirmation before development begins

### Development Process
- **Methodology:** AI-accelerated development using Claude-based tooling
- **Communication:** Client receives brief progress updates at agreed intervals (e.g., every 2–3 days or weekly, depending on project length)
- **Development Environment:** CrumbLabz manages all development infrastructure
- **Version Control:** All code is maintained in private repositories with full history

### Internal Development Workflow
1. Set up project repository and infrastructure
2. Build MVP features according to the prioritized scope
3. Internal QA and testing
4. Prepare demo environment for client review

---

## Phase 7: MVP Presentation & Business Agreement

### MVP Demo Meeting
- **Platform:** Google Meet with AI recording
- **Duration:** 30–60 minutes
- **Format:** Live demonstration of the working MVP in a staging environment

### Demo Agenda
1. **Walkthrough** — Demonstrate each MVP feature against the original problem statement
2. **Client Testing** — Allow the client to interact with the tool in real time
3. **Feedback Capture** — Document any issues, change requests, or questions
4. **Acceptance Discussion** — Confirm the MVP meets the agreed success criteria

### Business Agreement
- Upon client acceptance of the MVP, a formal agreement is executed
- **Invoicing:** Handled via Stripe or Square
  - Solution Sale: One-time invoice upon delivery acceptance
  - Monthly License: Recurring billing set up at agreed monthly rate
- **Agreement Terms:**
  - Scope of work delivered
  - Payment terms (Net 15 or Net 30, depending on client preference)
  - Support window (2 weeks included for Solution Sale; ongoing for Monthly License)
  - Intellectual property ownership (defined per engagement model)
  - Confidentiality and data handling provisions

### Post-Agreement
- Solution Sale: Final handoff package delivered (code, documentation, deployment guide)
- Monthly License: Solution deployed to production, CrumbLabz manages hosting and maintenance

---

## Phase 8: Ongoing Support & Future Development

### Our Answer Is Yes

CrumbLabz is built for long-term client relationships. When a client comes back with a new idea, an additional feature, or a completely new problem — the answer is always yes.

### Support Tiers

**For Solution Sale Clients:**
- 2-week post-delivery support window (bug fixes, minor adjustments)
- After the support window, additional work is scoped as a new engagement
- Clients are encouraged to convert to a Monthly License if ongoing needs emerge

**For Monthly License Clients:**
- Ongoing bug fixes and maintenance included
- Feature requests and enhancements prioritized and included in the monthly scope
- Priority response times
- Regular check-ins (monthly or quarterly) to review usage, gather feedback, and identify new opportunities

### Expansion Opportunities
- Each solved problem naturally surfaces adjacent problems
- CrumbLabz proactively identifies improvement opportunities during check-ins
- Additional tools can be added to an existing license or scoped as new engagements
- Referral program: satisfied clients are the best source of new business

## Guiding Principles

- **Speed over perfection.** Deliver a working MVP fast, then iterate. Clients value seeing progress over waiting for a polished spec.
- **Transparency at every step.** Clients always know where things stand. No black boxes, no surprise invoices.
- **Let AI handle the busywork.** Meeting transcripts, document drafts, and development acceleration — AI is embedded in the process so the team can focus on problem-solving and client relationships.
- **The answer is yes.** If a client has a problem, we find a way to solve it. Flexibility and willingness are competitive advantages.
- **Document everything.** Every meeting is recorded, every decision is captured. This protects both CrumbLabz and the client, and ensures nothing falls through the cracks.
