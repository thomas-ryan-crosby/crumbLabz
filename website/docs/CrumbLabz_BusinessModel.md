# CrumbLabz Business Model
Version 1.0

## Overview

CrumbLabz generates revenue by designing and building custom software tools that solve operational problems for businesses. The model is structured to minimize friction for potential clients by offering free upfront solution design, then converting engaged prospects into paying customers through one of two revenue paths.

## Engagement Flow

### Phase 1: Free Solution Design

The client describes an operational problem or inefficiency. CrumbLabz conducts a discovery process and produces a Solution Design Document at no cost to the client.

The Solution Design Document includes:
- A clear description of the problem
- An analysis of the current workflow
- A proposed technical solution
- Expected outcomes and improvements
- An estimated timeline for delivery

**Why free:** Offering solution design at no cost removes the biggest barrier to starting a conversation. Most businesses are hesitant to pay for consulting before they understand what they are getting. By delivering a concrete, thoughtful proposal upfront, CrumbLabz demonstrates competence, builds trust, and gives the client something tangible to evaluate — all before asking for a commitment.

This approach is especially valuable during the early growth phase as CrumbLabz builds its client base and reputation. It may be revisited as demand increases.

### Phase 2: Client Review

The client reviews the Solution Design Document. CrumbLabz walks through the proposal, answers questions, and aligns on scope and expectations. This is a collaborative step — the goal is to make sure the proposed solution genuinely fits the client's needs before any financial commitment.

### Phase 3: Revenue Conversion

After the client approves the solution design, CrumbLabz presents one of two commercial options depending on the nature of the solution and the client's preferences.

## Revenue Models

### Option A: Solution Sale (One-Time Purchase)

CrumbLabz builds the tool and delivers it to the client for a one-time project fee.

**Best for:**
- Solutions with a clearly defined scope
- Clients who have internal technical resources to maintain the tool
- Simpler tools that are unlikely to require ongoing iteration

**What the client receives:**
- The completed, working tool
- Documentation and handoff
- A short support window after delivery (e.g., 2 weeks)

**Pricing approach:**
- Scoped based on estimated effort, complexity, and value delivered
- Quoted as a fixed project fee after solution design approval

### Option B: Monthly Solution License (Recurring Revenue)

CrumbLabz builds, hosts, and maintains the tool for the client on an ongoing monthly subscription.

**Best for:**
- Solutions that benefit from ongoing improvements and iteration
- Clients who prefer not to manage technical infrastructure
- Tools that rely on integrations, data pipelines, or evolving workflows
- Longer-term client relationships

**What the client receives:**
- The working tool, hosted and managed by CrumbLabz
- Ongoing maintenance, updates, and bug fixes
- Priority support
- Iterative improvements as the client's needs evolve

**Pricing approach:**
- Monthly fee based on the scope and complexity of the solution
- Tiered if the solution involves usage-based components (e.g., data volume, user seats)
- No long-term contracts required — month-to-month by default

## Revenue Model Comparison

| | Solution Sale | Monthly License |
|---|---|---|
| Payment structure | One-time fee | Recurring monthly fee |
| Hosting & infrastructure | Client-managed | CrumbLabz-managed |
| Ongoing maintenance | Client responsibility (after support window) | Included |
| Iterative improvements | Scoped as separate engagements | Included |
| Best for | Defined, standalone tools | Evolving, integrated solutions |
| Revenue type | Project-based | Recurring |

## Strategic Notes

**Lead with licensing where possible.** Recurring revenue creates predictable income and deeper client relationships. Position the monthly license as the default recommendation, with the solution sale as an alternative for clients who prefer ownership.

**Free design is the growth engine.** The free Solution Design Document is the primary conversion tool. It should be high quality — not a generic template, but a genuinely useful analysis that demonstrates CrumbLabz's ability to understand and solve the client's problem. A strong design document sells itself.

**Upsell through trust.** Once a client has one working tool, they will naturally identify additional operational problems. Each solved problem strengthens the relationship and opens the door to additional solutions — either as new projects or added to an existing license.

**Track conversion metrics.** As the business matures, monitor:
- Number of solution designs produced
- Conversion rate from design to paid engagement
- Split between solution sales and monthly licenses
- Average revenue per client
- Client retention rate (for licensed solutions)

## Future Considerations

- **Productized solutions:** As CrumbLabz builds similar tools across clients, common solutions may be packaged into reusable products with standardized pricing.
- **Tiered licensing:** Introduce tiers (e.g., Basic, Pro, Enterprise) as solution complexity and client size varies.
- **Paid solution design:** As demand grows and brand trust is established, consider charging a nominal fee for solution design to filter serious prospects and value the work appropriately.
- **Retainer model:** For clients with multiple ongoing needs, offer a monthly retainer that covers a set number of development hours beyond a specific licensed solution.
